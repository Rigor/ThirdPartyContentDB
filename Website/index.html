Why Detect Third Party Content?

Modern websites contain more than 7 pieces of externally hosted third party content (3PC). Since the average webpage includes nearly 100 pieces of content, finding the 3PC your site uses can be difficult. This inability to detect 3PC creates two big problems:

  1. You can't manage your risk. Failures in 3PC can tank your entire web page and cause both lost visitors and lost sales. You need to know what 3PC you are using, and where it is on your site to manage the risk.
  2. The noise masks your problems. Testing and optimizing a website for performance or usability is hard enough already. Performance problems and bugs in 3PC that you can't fix or an unable to modify just adds noise and distration to the real quality and performance issues affecting your site. You need to identify 3PC so you can filter out this noise if need be.

Our Goal

Imagine a downloadable database or datafile containing with thousands of entries about 3PC like this: 

{
    'name': 'Google Analytics'
    'type': 'analytics'
    'homepage': 'http://www.google.com/analytics/'
    'patterns': ["/google-analytics\\.com\\/(urchin\\.js|ga\\.js)/i"]
}

and you can check to see if any URL references 3PC with a simply lookup layer like this: 

bool is3PC = ThirdPartyContentDB.is3PC("https://www.google-analytics.com/ga.js");

That is the Third Party Content database. Our goal is to build and maintain a simple, current, open source database of signatures that can detect third party content.

Open Source

We want a free, open database that anyone can use for any purpose.

Simple

The database should be simple, human readable, and not depend on [insert technology fad] here.

Offline

We don't want to be a service. The database should be simple and easy enough that it can be downloaded and used locally by anyone.

Language Agnostic

Our first priority is always to the data, and making it consumable and usable by any language.

Up-to-date

URLs change. New 3PC appears. We will create APIs and methods for the community to keep the database up-to-date with reliable signatures.
